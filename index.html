Stan Lee

A man who shaped the universe

Marvel Comics's primary creative leader

“With great power comes great responsibility.” ― Stan Lee

He co-created numerous popular fictional characters, including superheroes Spider-Man, the X-Men, Iron Man, Thor, the Hulk, the Fantastic Four, Black Panther, Daredevil, Doctor Strange, Scarlet Witch and Ant-Man.

In 1939: With the help of his uncle Robbie Solomon,Lee became an assistant at the new Timely Comics division of pulp magazine and comic-book publisher Martin Goodman's company.

In 1942: Lee entered the United States Army and served within the US as a member of the Signal Corps, repairing telegraph poles and other communications equipment.

In 1950s: The first superheroes Lee and artist Jack Kirby created together were the Fantastic Four, and further he created Hulk, Thor, Iron Man, X-Men,Doctor Strange and Marvel's most successful character Spider-Man.

In 1972: Lee stopped writing monthly comic books to assume the role of publisher.

In 1981: He moved to California to develop Marvel's TV and movie properties.

In 2011: Lee announced he would partner with 1821 Comics on a multimedia imprint for children

Nov 12, 2018: Lee died after being rushed there in a medical emergency that he had been battling pneumonia.
